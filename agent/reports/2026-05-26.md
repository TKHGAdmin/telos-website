First run of the bug hunter. Focused on functional issues in the most recently-touched code: the new shop / product / cart flow (`js/shop.js`), the Execution Score Quiz that gates pricing, and the email cron jobs. Three real bugs surfaced: the quiz lead-capture is fully bypassable (the pricing gate doesn't work), the daily engagement-reminder cron will spam inactive clients every day forever, and the weekly recap email's streak count is biased to undercount by one day every Monday. ## BUG-20260526-01 — Pricing gate is bypassable: quiz "See Your Score" accepts empty name + email

- **Severity:** P1
- **Area:** `js/quiz.js` (used by `index.html` and `pricing.html`)
- **Status:** new

**Reproduction**
1. Open https://www.telosathleticclub.com/pricing in an incognito window (no prior `localStorage`).
2. Click **Start the Diagnostic**, answer all 8 questions however you like (auto-advances).
3. On the "Your Score Is Ready" panel, leave **First name** and **Email address** blank.
4. Click **See Your Score**.
5. Results render. Click **View Pricing** at the bottom of the results.
6. Pricing is revealed in full. Refresh the page — pricing still shows, no overlay.

**Why it matters**
The quiz is the lead-capture gate for the pricing page — every reveal of the four tier prices is supposed to come with a name + email landing in `quiz_submissions` in Redis. Right now any visitor can click straight through with two empty fields and unlock pricing forever (the gate writes `telosQuizCompleted=true` to localStorage unconditionally). The lead never appears in the Leads tab, but the prospect sees pricing. That is both a lost lead and a misleading signal: the dashboard's "quiz completions" metric understates how many people are actually past the gate.

**Evidence**
- `js/quiz.js:225-275` — the click handler does `quiz.userName = nameInput.value` straight into the payload with no length / format check, then unconditionally sets `localStorage.setItem('telosQuizCompleted', 'true')` and reveals results.
- `index.html:580` and `pricing.html:594` — the button is `<button type="button" class="quiz-see-score-btn">`. Because it is `type="button"` and not `type="submit"`, the `required` attributes on the inputs at lines 577-578 / 591-592 are **never enforced** by the browser. HTML5 form validation only fires on a real submit (a `type="submit"` button or a `form.submit()` call).
- `pricing.html:971-977` — the gate is just `if (localStorage.getItem('telosQuizCompleted') === 'true')`, with no check on what was actually captured.
- Server-side, `api/submit-quiz.js:18-20` does reject empty name/email with a 400 — but the client swallows the rejection (`.catch(function() {})` at `js/quiz.js:269`) and proceeds to show results anyway.

**Suggested fix**
Inside the click handler in `js/quiz.js:225`, validate `nameInput.value.trim()` and a basic email regex on `emailInput.value.trim()` before doing anything else. If either fails, mark the input border red and return (don't set localStorage, don't POST, don't reveal results). Optional belt-and-suspenders: change the button to `type="submit"`, wrap the captured-state in `form.addEventListener('submit', ...)`, and `preventDefault()` — that re-enables the native `required` UI for free.

## BUG-20260526-02 — Engagement-reminder cron emails inactive clients every single day

- **Severity:** P1
- **Area:** `api/cron/engagement-check.js`
- **Status:** new

**Reproduction**
1. Make sure a portal-enabled, active client has not logged a daily check-in for 3+ days.
2. The cron at `0 15 * * *` runs once a day (see `vercel.json:25`).
3. On day 3 they receive "We miss you at Telos".
4. On day 4 — they receive it again.
5. On day 5, 6, 7, 8 ... they receive it again, every single day, until they log in or are removed from `clients_index`.

**Why it matters**
The intent of an engagement-reminder email is to nudge a wavering client once when they go quiet — not to mail them daily until they unsubscribe. After ~3-4 daily emails, most recipients mark the sender as spam, which torches Resend deliverability for the **weekly summary** emails going to active clients too. It's a quiet but compounding reputation risk.

**Evidence**
- `api/cron/engagement-check.js:60-94` — the trigger condition is just `if (lastLogDaysAgo === -1 || lastLogDaysAgo >= 3)`, with no record of when the last reminder was sent. Every cron tick that finds the client still inactive re-sends.
- The lookback window is only 5 days (line 45), so beyond day 5 of inactivity `lastLogDaysAgo` is permanently `-1`, the email always says "It's been 5+ days", and it goes out every 24 hours forever.
- Compare to `weekly-summary.js`, which is naturally throttled by its weekly schedule — but that one too has no idempotency record.

**Suggested fix**
Before sending, read `client_engagement_last_sent:{clientId}` (or stash on the client object), and only send if it's been ≥7 days since the previous reminder. Set the key with `SET` (TTL 30 days) immediately after a successful Resend POST. Two lines of code; kills the spam loop. As a bonus, cap total reminders at 2-3 per inactivity stretch to avoid annoying clients who are simply on vacation.

## BUG-20260526-03 — Weekly recap streak count undercounts by 1 every Monday morning

- **Severity:** P2
- **Area:** `api/cron/weekly-summary.js`
- **Status:** new

**Reproduction**
1. A client logs a daily check-in every day Sun → Sat (7 days straight).
2. Monday at 14:00 UTC (9am ET) the weekly-summary cron fires.
3. The client has not yet logged today (Monday) because they're still asleep / commuting.
4. The "Current Streak" line in their recap email reads **0 days**, despite having a 7-day streak going into Monday.

**Why it matters**
The streak number is the most motivating piece of the recap email — it's what gets clients to keep showing up. Resetting it to 0 every Monday morning because they haven't logged yet (when the cron runs **before** most people start their day) is actively demoralising and will look like a Telos bug, not a streak break, to the client. Less severe than #1/#2 but it hits every active client every Monday.

**Evidence**
- `api/cron/weekly-summary.js:86-90`:
  ```js
  // Count streak (from today backwards)
  for (var d = 0; d < 7; d++) {
    if (weekData[0][d] && weekData[0][d].result) streak++;
    else break;
  }
  ```
  `d=0` is today (UTC). If today has no log yet the loop breaks immediately and `streak` stays at 0, regardless of consecutive prior days.
- The cron schedule is `0 14 * * 1` (`vercel.json:21`) — 9am ET Monday, which is before the bulk of clients open the app.

**Suggested fix**
Start the streak count at `d=1` (yesterday) and add today (`d=0`) on top only if it's also logged. One-line change:
```js
var startD = (weekData[0][0] && weekData[0][0].result) ? 0 : 1;
for (var d = startD; d < 7; d++) {
  if (weekData[0][d] && weekData[0][d].result) streak++;
  else break;
}
```
Same fix should be ported to the client-dashboard streak calc when the documented `todayStr()` UTC bug gets addressed.
