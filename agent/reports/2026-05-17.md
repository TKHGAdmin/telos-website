## BUG-2026-05-17-01: engagement-check cron re-sends "we miss you" email every day to every inactive client

- **Severity:** P2
- **Area:** api/cron
- **File(s):** api/cron/engagement-check.js:60-94, vercel.json:23-26
- **Reproduction:** The cron is scheduled at `0 15 * * *` (daily 15:00 UTC) in `vercel.json`. For each active, portal-enabled client it looks back 5 days of `client_dailylog:{id}:{date}` and computes `lastLogDaysAgo`. If `lastLogDaysAgo === -1 || lastLogDaysAgo >= 3` it POSTs an email to Resend (lines 61-89). Nothing in the handler reads or writes a "last engagement email sent" timestamp, and `grep -r 'last_email\|lastEmailSent\|reminder'` across `api/` returns no matches. As a result, a client whose last check-in was 5 days ago receives one engagement email today, another tomorrow, another the day after - the same `daysSince = 5` body each time (line 62 caps `daysSince` at 5 because the lookup window is 5 days) - and it never stops until they log in again.
- **Impact:** Inactive clients get the same "It's been 5+ days since your last check-in" email every day they remain inactive. The text is identical across consecutive days because `daysSince` is capped at 5. Real-world effects: (a) user annoyance / unsubscribe risk, (b) spam-marking risk on `noreply@telosathleticclub.com`, which would degrade deliverability of password resets, weekly summaries, and CHS application follow-ups, since all three flow through the same Resend sender, (c) wasted Resend quota.
- **Suggested fix:** Track `client.engagementEmailSentAt` (or a separate Redis key with a TTL) and skip if the last email was sent within N days; or only send when `lastLogDaysAgo === 3` so each inactivity streak triggers exactly one reminder.
- **Confidence:** high
